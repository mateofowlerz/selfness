# Why not choosing life?

I've met a girl who might be the mother of my children. I usually don't like people, even though I meet a lot of them, so every time someone feels interesting I open my eyes. The problem? She lives 10,000 kilometers away.

This got me thinking: why the fuck do I always end up in unconventional, suffering-prone, high-risk paths in life? Why I'm constantly buying problems for myself?

I've chosen to start a startup at 17 years old without knowing shit about practically anything. I've chosen to do so in America, a country I know nothing about and where, given that this is a B2C startup, culture is important. I could have stayed in Argentina and used my competitive advantage in my favor. I could have avoided suffering for a year to get my visa. I could have decided not to build a business at all, or at least to build a safer one. I guess I could have decided not to fall in love with a European either.

I really can extrapolate this to multiple decisions I've made in all games I play in life. I've always had a bias towards high-risk things, towards weird people and towards the dark and miserable outcomes in life. I hate the common. I have an irrational sense of difference for the sake of it. I "enjoy" the conflict and the suffering that these endeavors bring.
If I feel a thought is dangerous or dark, I pursue it. I like conversations with people who live unconventional, often miserable lives. I've listened to Uber drivers talk about their miserable lives for hours and, for some reason, I enjoy that.
I sense that my low risk aversion is somewhat related to my interest in dark things. I can't understand why or put it into clear words.

I feel repulsion toward the other side of things. I hate classic happy-people small talk. I disguise ![a normal house with a normal family and a shiny 4x4 parked at the front](/images/pasted-image-20260324200448.png). I dislike everything that drifts towards the average, but I recognize the superiority of average outcomes given the path to the extraordinary ones.

Every time I reason about this, I find myself thinking that I should be pursuing the happy, normal path because that's the rational thing to do. But my body gets sick at the sole idea of dying and looking back on my life having been an average happy person.

This prompts the question: what the fuck is wrong with me, and with everyone who feels compelled by what I've just described?

My reasoning behind this makes me feel that we're the ones that are wrong. I see people who live like this and I only sense **pure happiness** in them. Why aren't we pursuing that?
